# Product

## Register

product

## Users
Primary users are Business Analysts working in fast-moving discovery and solution-shaping workflows. They need to capture process flows, clarify stakeholder handoffs, structure requirements visually, and iterate quickly between text and diagram form without losing fidelity. Secondary users include systems analysts and solution architects who review, refine, or extend those diagrams into more technical system flows.

## Product Purpose
This product is a browser-based Business Analyst workbench for creating, editing, and evolving flow diagrams with a tight loop between visual editing and Mermaid code. Its purpose is to reduce the friction between eliciting requirements, mapping current and future processes, and turning messy analysis into shareable structured artifacts. Success means a BA can move from rough draft to clear, editable process map quickly, with the product feeling fast, dependable, and easy to understand even at MVP scope.

## Brand Personality
Clear, practical, supportive. The product should feel like a focused BA workspace, not a flashy AI toy or a generic white-label diagram editor. It should help users think, structure, and communicate with confidence while staying lightweight and friendly.

## Anti-references
Do not make this feel like a noisy whiteboard clone, a crypto-style AI cockpit, or a dense enterprise admin panel full of visual chrome. Avoid cluttered multi-toolbar experiences, generic "AI assistant" chat-first layouts, and overly decorative productivity UI that slows down expert work. The MVP should stay compact, fast to render, and obvious to use.

## Design Principles
1. Keep visual analysis and structured source close together, so users can move between code and canvas without context switching.
2. Optimize for the core BA loop first: capture, refine, validate, and share process understanding.
3. Make important actions predictable and visible, especially save, sync, export, and collaboration steps.
4. Prepare the surface for future automation and agent assistance, but never let speculative AI features overshadow the primary editing workflow.
5. Favor speed, clarity, and low-friction interaction over ornament or novelty, especially at MVP stage.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target a solid MVP usability baseline with clear labels, readable contrast, keyboard-friendly primary actions where practical, and restrained motion. Prioritize user-friendly interaction and fast rendering over advanced visual effects. Additional accessibility requirements can be layered in later as the product matures.
